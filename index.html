About
Eunseong Song
Designer / Visual Storyteller

I am a designer who explores how visuals shape perception, emotion, and meaning. My work focuses on the space between what is seen and what is understood, using typography, motion, and composition to create layered experiences. Rather than simply presenting information, I aim to translate ideas into visuals that communicate on both an immediate and intuitive level.

Across my projects, I experiment with contrast: clarity and distortion, structure and chaos, emotion and function. Through this process, I explore how design can move beyond aesthetics and become a tool for storytelling, branding, and user experience.

01
Assignment 1
DON'T BE BORING / WATCH THAT KERNING

DON'T BE BORING

Description

A typography poster built around the phrases “Don't be boring” and “Watch that kerning.” The composition uses a bowling visual pun to connect boring and bowling, while manipulated spacing turns typography into part of the message.

Intention

I wanted to show that typography is not just about readability, but about perception. Even a small detail like kerning can completely change how a design feels and communicates.

02
Assignment 2
REDESIGN LOGOS OR MASHUPS

Different Ring, Same Obsession

Description

A logo mashup combining the Mercedes-Benz ring with the Jordan Jumpman silhouette, developed into an animated lockup. The slogan “Different ring, same obsession” connects luxury engineering and athletic excellence through a shared mindset.

Intention

I wanted to explore how two very different brands could be unified by one value: obsession with performance. The ring becomes both a logo form and a championship symbol.

03
Assignment 3
CUSTOM AD

The Path to Obsession

Description

A custom advertisement built from Michael Jordan game footage. The basketball hoop is composited into the Mercedes × Jordan merged logo, while narration focuses on the difficult path toward success.

Intention

This project translates brand identity into a story about discipline, struggle, and precision. I wanted the logo to function as a symbolic goal rather than just a graphic mark.

04
Assignment 4
SWEETHEARTS AD

Let Your Heart Be Visible

Description

A Sweethearts animation where, instead of hearts appearing in someone's eyes, Sweethearts candies burst out. The logo and slogan turn a familiar romantic visual into a playful branded moment.

Intention

I wanted to turn emotion into product language and show how branding can feel natural when it grows from a relatable human reaction.

05
Assignment 5
MOVIE / SHOW TITLE

The Umbrella Academy Title Sequence

Description

A title sequence for The Umbrella Academy built around a rotating black-and-white spiral. Each character is introduced through the same hypnotic visual system, with multiply masking used to create an eerie, unstable atmosphere.

Intention

I wanted to capture the strange and psychologically distorted tone of the show through repetition, motion, and visual ambiguity.

06
Assignment 6
IDC

Wave to Earth Lyrics Poster

Description

An experimental lyric poster inspired by David Carson's typographic language. Layered text, rough textures, and fragmented image placement prioritize atmosphere over clean readability.

Intention

I wanted the poster to feel like the song sounds: emotional, raw, and slightly disorienting. Meaning arrives through mood as much as through words.

07
Assignment 7
F1 HELMET PRODUCT PAGE

Ignition Blade X

Description

A conceptual Red Bull F1 helmet merged with other brand identities and presented through a polished product page layout. The design uses sharp metallic forms and flame-based graphics to suggest ignition, speed, and control.

Intention

I wanted to combine product design, branding, and presentation into one believable concept that feels both speculative and market-ready.

08
Assignment 8
INSTRUCTIONAL VIDEO / BROCHURE

How to Make a Perfect Sunny Side Up Egg

Description

A step-by-step brochure explaining how to cook a sunny side up egg, supported by soft illustrations, approachable typography, and a GIF that makes the process easier to understand through motion.

Intention

I wanted to show that good design is also functional: clear, warm, and helpful. The GIF extends the brochure from static information into usable instruction.
